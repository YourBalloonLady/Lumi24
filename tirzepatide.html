Tirzepatide Peptide Guide

Tirzepatide is a synthetic peptide that functions as a dual agonist for both the GLP-1 (glucagon-like peptide-1) and GIP (glucose-dependent insulinotropic polypeptide) receptors. By activating these pathways, tirzepatide has become a major subject of research in metabolic regulation, appetite signaling, and weight management studies.

Researchers investigate tirzepatide for its ability to influence blood glucose regulation, energy metabolism, and appetite control. Because it targets two incretin hormone pathways simultaneously, it represents a unique advancement in peptide-based metabolic research.

How Tirzepatide Works

Tirzepatide activates GLP-1 and GIP receptors, two hormones involved in glucose metabolism and appetite regulation. These hormones help regulate insulin secretion, slow gastric emptying, and signal satiety to the brain.

Through these combined mechanisms, tirzepatide may influence energy balance, metabolic efficiency, and body weight regulation in research models.
