Frequently asked questions

Some compounds, such as NAD+ and SLU-PP-332, are not peptides in the strictest sense but are often discussed alongside peptide research because they overlap with metabolism, cellular energy, recovery or longevity topics.

No. The page is educational only. It does not diagnose, treat, recommend, prescribe, advise human use, or provide dosing or administration instructions.

Online discussion often moves faster than formal research. Some compounds have stronger clinical literature, while others are supported mainly by early-stage, animal, mechanistic or anecdotal discussion. A good research library should make that difference clear.

The most useful starting points are usually Retatrutide, Tirzepatide, Semaglutide, Cagrilintide, MOTS-C, SS-31, SLU-PP-332, CJC-1295 + Ipamorelin, Tesamorelin, Selank, Semax and the BPC-157/TB-500 blend.
